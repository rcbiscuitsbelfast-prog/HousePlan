## NI Case Studies

### Clifton House (1774), Grade A listed, north Belfast
- **Owner:** Clifton House Trust (CCNI-registered charitable company)
- **Funders:** NLHF, AHF, Historic Environment Division NI
- **Est. total investment:** £2m–£4m across multiple campaigns [3]
- **Lesson:** NLHF funds across multiple campaigns. Charitable trust governance is essential. Grade A listing = priority HED NI access.

### Duncairn Centre (2014), Grade B1, north Belfast — capacity 170
- **Owner:** Duncairn Community Partnership (charity)
- **Funders:** NLHF, SEUPB PEACE IV, BCC, Arts Council NI
- **Est. project cost:** £3m–£4m [4]
- **Lesson:** NLHF + SEUPB combination proven in Belfast. Cross-community narrative unlocks SEUPB.

### Crumlin Road Gaol (1846), Grade B+, north Belfast — 150,000+ visitors/yr
- **Owner:** Private commercial operator
- **Funders:** NLHF 2011 award [5][6]
- **Lesson:** Heritage tourism viable in Belfast. Private operation = locked out of future public capital. Charitable model preserves ongoing grant access.
