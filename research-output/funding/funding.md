*funding-agent v2.0.0 | 2026-05-08 | POPULATED with live research — DO NOT OVERWRITE*

## Key Finding

Fernhill sits at the intersection of three of the most fundable narratives in current UK and NI heritage policy: **building at risk, community benefit, and cross-community reconciliation**. This unlocks a wider funding mix than almost any other type of project.

---

## Primary Funders

### 1. National Lottery Heritage Fund (NLHF)
**URL:** heritagefund.org.uk | **NI:** heritagefund.org.uk/in-your-area/northern-ireland

**Track record in NI:** Over **£290 million awarded since 1994 to almost 2,000 projects** in Northern Ireland. [1]

| Programme | Range | Match | Window | Fernhill Target |
|-----------|-------|-------|--------|----------------|
| National Lottery Project Grants | £10k–£250k | 10% (in-kind ok) | Rolling | Phase A works or community engagement |
| **Heritage Places** | **£250k–£10m+** | **5–10%** | **Two-stage** | **£800k–£1.5m for Phase B** |

**Contact NI:** 4th Floor, 1 Lanyon Place, Belfast BT1 3LP

---

### 2. Architectural Heritage Fund (AHF)
**URL:** ahfund.org.uk/grants/northern-ireland/ [2]

NI-specific programme. **FIRST APPLICATION TO SUBMIT.**

| Programme | Range | Match | Window |
|-----------|-------|-------|--------|
| **Project Development Grant** | **£10k–£100k** | **None** | **Rolling — no deadline** |
| Viability and Capacity Grant | Up to £25k | None | Rolling |

**Fernhill target: £50,000–£80,000 PDG** to fund professional building survey, business plan, and architectural design.

---

### 3. Historic Environment Fund NI
**Body:** Department for Communities, NI Executive
**URL:** communities-ni.gov.uk — launched by Communities Minister Gordon Lyons [3]

- Supports listed building repair, conservation area works, heritage-at-risk projects
- Application via Historic Environment Division (HED), Department for Communities
- **Fernhill target: £100,000–£400,000 for Phase A and B structural works**

---

### 4. SEUPB PEACE PLUS
**Body:** Special EU Programmes Body (SEUPB), delivered via Belfast City Council
**Belfast allocation: £15.1 million (€17.4 million)** to Belfast PEACEPLUS Local Community Action Plan [4]

- Key requirement: **cross-community benefit must be demonstrated**
- Fernhill's proposed model (serving all Belfast communities for weddings, wakes, community events) is a near-perfect fit
- **Fernhill target: £100,000–£300,000** for community spaces, remembrance garden, cross-community programming
- Apply via: belfastcity.gov.uk/council/equality-and-diversity/peaceplus

## Recommended Stacking Strategy

### Phase 1 — Feasibility (Target: £80,000–£160,000)

| Funder | Target |
|--------|--------|
| AHF Project Development Grant | £50,000–£80,000 |
| Belfast City Council community grant | £15,000–£30,000 |
| CFNI donor-advised fund | £10,000–£30,000 |
| Pilgrim Trust | £5,000–£20,000 |

### Phase 2 — Capital Restoration (Target: £1,200,000–£2,200,000)

| Funder | Target | Role |
|--------|--------|------|
| NLHF Heritage Places | £800k–£1.5m | Anchor |
| Historic Environment Fund NI | £150k–£400k | Listed building layer |
| SEUPB PEACE PLUS (via BCC) | £100k–£300k | Cross-community layer |
| Wolfson / Garfield Weston / Pilgrim | £100k–£300k | Philanthropic match |
| American Ireland Fund | £50k–£150k | Diaspora fundraising |
| Community shares / Spacehive | £50k–£100k | Community ownership |
| Charity Bank loan | £100k–£200k | Bridge |

**Critical principle:** Build the match package first. Submit NLHF Stage 1 with confirmed or near-confirmed match in place.

**Target stacking ratio:** 65% public grants / 20% philanthropy and diaspora / 10% social investment / 5% community shares
