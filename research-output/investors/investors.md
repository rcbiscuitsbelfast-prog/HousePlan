# Investor & Philanthropic Funder Mapping — Fernhill House

**Version:** 3.0  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Status:** Populated — programme references verified; individual donor cultivation remains project-specific  

---

## Executive summary

Fernhill’s capital stack should combine **public heritage grants** (anchor), **philanthropic match**, **place-based NI funds**, and — only if required — **patient social finance**. This document maps non-grant and match-funding sources. Public grant logic is in `funding-roadmap-v3.md` and `funding/funding.json`.

**Recommended sequence:** secure AHF NI development funding → build match table → approach NLHF with confirmed philanthropic and community layers.

## 3. Social investment (bridge only)

|----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Charity Bank** | Loan £50k–£3m+ | Bridge between grant award and build cashflow | https://www.charitybank.org |
| **Ulster Community Investment Trust** | NI social loan | Fit-out or working capital if subsidiary cashflow supports | https://www.ucit.co.uk |

**Principle:** Do not rely on debt for core capital unless grant drawdown timing creates a short, secured bridge with clear repayment from confirmed grants.

## 4. Corporate sponsorship & CSR

**Model:** Tiered sponsorship (garden, room, programme year) plus in-kind professional support.

| Tier | Indicative ask | Benefits |
|------|---------------|----------|
| Principal partner | £25,000+ | Naming, launch visibility, board access |
| Heritage partner | £10,000–£24,999 | Event sponsorship, signage |
| Community partner | £2,000–£9,999 | Local recognition, ticket bundles |

**Cultivation list (desk research required per firm):** Belfast construction/heritage contractors; solicitors with charity practices; regional banks; utilities with NI community funds.
