## Executive summary

Fernhill House (HB Ref HB26/38/004A), Glencairn Park, Glencairn Road, Belfast BT13 3PT, is a listed Victorian villa within west Belfast’s park network [S01]. The building has significant heritage value and has experienced extended vacancy; deterioration risk increases without phased stabilisation and repair.

**Strategic proposition.** The project advanced in companion documentation proposes a **community-led charitable vehicle** operating a multi-stream venue: heritage-led daytime use (tea room, interpretation, community hire), life-event hospitality (weddings, wakes), and complementary programming. That model aligns with established Northern Ireland funding narratives (heritage at risk, community benefit, and — where evidenced in governance and programming — cross-community use).

**Planning and heritage.** Works to a listed building require **planning permission** and **listed building consent** where applicable, with early engagement with the Historic Environment Division (HED) and the local planning authority. The site sits within **Glencairn Park**; any physical changes to access, parking, or landscape must be tested against relevant development plan policies and park/greenspace objectives.

**Capital.** Parametric capital estimates in supporting materials suggest an **all-in** range in the order of **£1.6m–£2.9m** including contingency and professional fees, subject to condition survey. Phasing (stabilisation → core repair → fit-out) is the standard delivery route for vacant heritage assets.

**Revenue.** Illustrative operating projections in the master plan suggest potential sustainability at maturity subject to **capacity management**, **staffing model**, and **catering/bar choices**. These projections must be rebuilt in a spreadsheet model with named assumptions and sensitivity cases.

**Funding.** A stacked approach is appropriate: **Architectural Heritage Fund** (development/feasibility), **National Lottery Heritage Fund** (larger capital — subject to programme rules at application), **Historic Environment Fund NI** and other public/philanthropic layers as eligibility allows, plus match from trusts, community fundraising, and — if required — prudently structured social finance.

**Conclusion.** The project is **feasible to take forward** as a structured regeneration proposition provided that (1) statutory and technical risks are addressed through early engagement and survey, (2) transport and parking assumptions are field-verified, (3) governance and operations are defined clearly enough for grant due diligence, and (4) financial cases are presented as scenarios, not certainties.

## Access, Transport & Connectivity Strategy

### Overview

Fernhill House benefits from a significantly stronger transport and accessibility position than many comparable heritage venues in Northern Ireland. Located within Glencairn Park in West Belfast, the site already sits within an established network of roads, pedestrian routes, park infrastructure, and public transport links.

The proposal intentionally adopts a **distributed and landscape-sensitive transport strategy**, avoiding excessive hard landscaping or suburban-style car park expansion while still supporting viable daily operations and event use.

This approach aligns with:

* sustainable transport principles;
* heritage and parkland conservation objectives;
* Belfast City Council green-space policies;
* active travel and accessibility goals;
* Glencairn Place Shaping Plan priorities focused on connectivity, inclusion, and improved public amenity.

---

# 1. Vehicular Access

Fernhill House is accessed directly from Glencairn Road/Forthriver Road via existing park and estate routes already used by:

* park visitors;
* play park users;
* maintenance vehicles;
* local residents;
* previous museum-era operations.

No major new vehicular infrastructure is currently anticipated.

Existing road infrastructure surrounding the site is considered suitable for:

* daily café/tea room use;
* heritage visitation;
* community events;
* wakes and remembrance functions;
* small-to-medium weddings and private events.

The proposal therefore focuses on upgrading and managing existing access arrangements rather than creating extensive new road systems.

---

# 2. Existing Parking Provision

### Existing Estate Parking

The former Fernhill estate parking area currently appears capable of accommodating approximately:

* **25 vehicles minimum** (subject to survey and layout optimisation).

This area is suitable for:

* staff parking;
* daily visitors;
* tea room operations;
* wakes/funeral teas;
* community meetings;
* accessible visitor parking;
* operational servicing.

Importantly, this parking infrastructure already exists within the site context, reducing planning and landscape impacts.

### Adjacent Existing Public Parking

#### Play Park Parking

An additional public parking area adjacent to the Glencairn play park provides approximately:

* **10 marked spaces**.

This supports:

* shared daytime usage;
* family visitors;
* overflow parking during events;
* wider park integration.

---

#### Southern Park Access Bays

A smaller group of existing park-access spaces south of the site provides:

* approximately **7 additional spaces**.

These contribute to:

* distributed visitor access;
* flexible overflow arrangements;
* operational resilience during peak periods.

---

### Overflow & Flexible Parking Potential

An area of existing grassland/tree-edge space west/south-west of the estate has been identified as a potential location for:

* accessible parking;
* event-day overflow parking;
* permeable reinforced grass systems;
* shuttle/drop-off infrastructure.

This area would be approached sensitively using:

* low-impact materials;
* permeable surfaces;
* landscape screening;
* biodiversity-conscious design;
* event-only operational use where appropriate.

At this stage, no major permanent car park expansion is proposed.

## 3. Site and statutory context

### 3.1 Address and listing

Fernhill House is recorded at **Glencairn Park, Glencairn Road, Belfast BT13 3PT**; listing grade **B2** (Survey 2); extent **House** [S01]. Associated outbuildings are recorded separately on the register. Any works must respect curtilage and group value identified in the statutory record.

### 3.2 Setting

The statutory description notes an elevated setting within parkland, approached from Glencairn Road, with outbuildings and boundary walls forming part of the group. Operational proposals must treat **landscape, trees, and boundaries** as part of the heritage asset, not as peripheral space.

### 3.3 Principal planning considerations

- **Listed building consent** and **planning permission** for material changes.
- **Parkland / open space** context — intensification of use may require careful justification and design quality.
- **Access and parking** — numbers in Section 2 remain subject to measured survey and officer engagement.

## 7. Sustainability strategy

- **Fabric first:** repair roofs, drains, and thermal performance sympathetically to reduce energy demand.
- **Services:** efficient heating strategy compatible with listed constraints; monitoring and maintenance contracts.
- **Water:** leak detection; sensible catering equipment specification.
- **Waste:** segregated waste for events; local supply chains where practical.
- **Biodiversity:** retain mature trees; avoid unnecessary hardstanding; consider dark-skies sensitive external lighting.
- **Travel:** align operations with Section 2 (distributed model, active travel improvements).

## 9. Heritage impact summary

**Significance:** Victorian villa with quality exterior composition and surviving historic fabric; associations documented in the statutory record.

**Approach:** repair over replacement; minimal loss of historic fabric; new work distinguishable and reversible where practicable; documentary recording before alteration.

**Deliverables for consent / funding:** Heritage Impact Assessment / statement; method statements for sensitive trades; conservation-specification schedules.

## 10. Operational realism

### 10.1 User groups and flows

Distinct cohorts (daily visitors, wedding parties, wake attendees, community users) require **separated flows and scheduling** to avoid conflict — west wing / main house / outbuilding logic should be tested in design.

### 10.2 Catering model decision

Options: in-house kitchen; approved external caterers; hybrid. This choice drives capital (kitchen size), licensing, staffing, and margin — **must be fixed before final business case**.

### 10.3 Staffing

Core roles plus casual event staff are typical. Peak weekends require scale plans commensurate with fire, licensing, and safeguarding responsibilities.
