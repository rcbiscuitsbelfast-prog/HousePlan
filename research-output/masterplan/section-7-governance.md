# Section 7: Governance & Legal Structure

**Status:** Complete (v3.0) | **Date:** May 2026 | **Evidence:** `research-output/governance/governance.json`

---

## 7.1 Recommendation

**Adopt a Charitable Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG)** registered with **Charity Commission for Northern Ireland (CCNI)**, with a **wholly-owned private limited trading subsidiary** for commercial operations.

**Suitability:** VERY HIGH — standard model for NLHF, AHF, and HEF NI eligibility.

---

## 7.2 Why not a CIC alone?

Community Interest Companies are **not eligible** for NLHF, AHF, or HEF NI capital grants and cannot claim Gift Aid. A CIC may be useful in other contexts but is **not recommended** as the primary vehicle for Fernhill.

---

## 7.3 Hybrid structure (recommended)

```
Fernhill Heritage Trust (CLG, CCNI registered)
  │— owns building & land interest
  │— receives grants & donations
  │— delivers charitable programmes
  │
  └── Fernhill Trading Ltd (100% subsidiary)
        │— weddings, wakes, tea room, hire
        │— employs operational staff
        └── year-end profit → deed of covenant → charity
```

**Benefits:**

- Maximum grant eligibility  
- Gift Aid on donations  
- Commercial flexibility for hospitality  
- Corporation tax neutral via covenant (subject to tax advice)  
- Risk separation between charity and trading  

**NI precedent:** Clifton House Trust, Belfast — CCNI charitable company managing Grade A listed asset with NLHF funding.

## 7.4 Trustee board composition (recommended 7–9)

| Seat | Expertise |
|------|-----------|
| Chair | Governance + stakeholder management |
| Heritage / conservation | HED-facing literacy |
| Finance | Charity accounts, reserves |
| Legal / property | Lease, asset transfer |
| Community | West Belfast legitimacy |
| Commercial / hospitality | Operations realism |
| Fundraising | Trust relationships |

**Project lead** may serve as CEO/operations director but should not dominate board votes without clear conflicts policy.

---

## 7.5 Registration steps

1. Incorporate CLG at Companies House  
2. Apply for CCNI registration with public benefit statement  
3. Incorporate trading subsidiary  
4. Adopt conflicts, safeguarding, and reserves policies  
5. Open charity bank account + restricted funds  

**Solicitors (NI charity practice):** Edwards, Cleaver Fulton Rankin, Carson McDowell — see `supplier-ecosystem-map.md`.

---

## 7.6 Asset transfer

Critical path item: **legal agreement with Belfast City Council** (or current owner) for lease, licence, or asset transfer. Structure affects:

- Business rates relief (80% mandatory charity relief)  
- Insurance  
- Planning responsibility  
- Funder comfort on tenure
