**Status:** Complete (v3.0) | **Date:** May 2026 | **Evidence:** `research-output/restoration/restoration.json`

---

## 5.1 Condition challenge

Fernhill House is **Grade B2 listed** and has experienced **extended vacancy**. Typical risks for Victorian villas in this condition include:

- Roof and chimney failure → water ingress  
- Timber decay (floors, windows, roof structure)  
- Masonry movement and failed pointing  
- Obsolete M&E and fire safety non-compliance  

**First action:** RICS building survey + structural appraisal — non-negotiable before capital fundraising.

---

## 5.2 Conservation principles

1. **Repair before replace** — retain historic fabric  
2. **Reversible interventions** where new work is required  
3. **Traditional materials** — lime mortar, natural slate, timber sash  
4. **Minimal harm** to setting within Glencairn Park  
5. **Phased delivery** — stabilise before fit-out

## 5.3 Elemental cost benchmarks (indicative)

| Element | Unit cost range | Source type |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| Natural slate reroofing | £150–£250/m² | Industry benchmark 2024 |
| Lime repointing | £40–£85/m² | Heritage contractor guides |
| Sash window overhaul | £600–£1,200/window | Specialist joinery |
| Full M&E replacement | £25–£45/m² electrical | QS estimate required |
| Accessibility package | £30,000–£80,000 | Platform lift, WC, ramps |

**Listed building uplift:** +25–40% over standard construction for specialist labour, materials, and programme risk.
