## 2. Architectural significance

Fernhill House is a **Victorian villa** within a designed parkland setting. Grade B2 listing indicates a building of special architectural or historic interest where more than special interest would be required for Grade B1 or A.

**Typical significance themes for this building type:**

- Victorian domestic architecture and plan form  
- Survival of parkland relationship between house and grounds  
- Material palette (masonry, slate, timber sash windows, decorative plaster)  
- Group value within Glencairn Park landscape  

**Conservation principle:** Repair over replacement; reversible interventions; minimal harm to historic fabric; distinguish new work from old.

---

## 3. Historical & social significance

Fernhill has served multiple chapters of Belfast’s social history (private residence, institutional/museum use, period of vacancy). A full **Statement of Significance** should be completed by a conservation architect using:

- HED/NI Buildings Register data  
- Historic maps and photographs  
- Oral history from local residents  
- Archives relating to museum period and community memory  

**Interpretation opportunity:** Place-based storytelling linking Victorian Belfast, parkland civic amenity, and contemporary community reuse — without privileging a single political narrative.

---

## 4. Setting significance

The house sits within **Glencairn Park**, an active public green space with play facilities, walking routes, and connections to wider Belfast Hills / greenway networks. Any works must respect:

- Tree and root protection  
- Park character and biodiversity  
- Distributed parking strategy (see feasibility transport section)  
- Public access and safety  

---

## 5. Heritage at risk

Extended vacancy increases:

- Water ingress and timber decay  
- Security and anti-social behaviour risk  
- Loss of historic fabric through neglect  

**Phased stabilisation (Phase A)** is standard good practice before full restoration.
