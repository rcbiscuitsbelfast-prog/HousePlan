# R.C. Master Action List

**Purpose:** Practical things R.C. needs to do, in order.  
**Rule:** Do not jump to public fundraising before survey/council/funder route is credible.

---

## First 7 Days

1. Create a project folder with:
   - One-page brief.
   - Two-page brief.
   - Full v3 masterplan.
   - Statutory listing record.
   - Action log.
2. Write a list of 10 possible steering group members.
3. Choose 3 priority skill gaps:
   - Charity/governance.
   - Finance.
   - Heritage/buildings.
4. Prepare a short email to councillors asking for officer introductions.
5. Start an engagement register with date, person, organisation, ask, response, next step.

---

## Days 8-30

1. Contact councillors covering the relevant Glencairn/Castle DEA context.
2. Ask for introductions to:
   - Belfast City Council Parks.
   - Estates/property.
   - Planning.
   - Heritage/conservation.
   - Regeneration/community planning.
3. Contact AHF NI and ask:
   - Is Fernhill eligible for current NI viability/development support?
   - What legal structure is needed before application?
   - What survey and consultant costs are eligible?
4. Contact 3 conservation architects for a short initial scoping call.
5. Ask each architect:
   - What survey package is needed first?
   - What would a 6-8 week feasibility scope cost?
   - What are the biggest listed building risks?
6. Start quiet community listening:
   - 3 community groups.
   - 2 clergy/faith contacts.
   - 2 funeral directors.
   - 1 school contact.

---

## Days 31-60

1. Hold the first council officer meeting.
2. Confirm who owns/manages:
   - Estate parking.
   - Park access routes.
   - Play park parking.
   - Southern bays.
   - Potential overflow area.
3. Request guidance on:
   - Event-day transport evidence.
   - Listed building consent route.
   - Public consultation expectations.
   - Any council capital programme overlap.
4. Shortlist consultant team.
5. Prepare AHF application scope.
6. Draft steering group terms of reference.

---

## Days 61-90

1. Submit or prepare AHF viability/development application.
2. Commission condition survey if funding/permissions allow.
3. Build a first financial spreadsheet:
   - Base case.
   - Downside.
   - Upside.
4. Prepare NLHF early project enquiry notes.
5. Create consultation plan:
   - Who to contact.
   - What to ask.
   - How responses will influence the design.
6. Decide whether the next public step is:
   - Site walkover.
   - Community meeting.
   - Online survey.
   - Small stakeholder roundtable.

## What to Say in First Meetings

Use this script:

> I am developing a community-led feasibility route for Fernhill House in Glencairn Park. The aim is to restore the listed building as a heritage and community asset with carefully managed earned income from tea room use, community hire, wakes, and small-to-medium events. I am not asking anyone to approve a final scheme yet. I am asking for the right officer route, the right survey evidence, and guidance on what would be needed to test feasibility properly.

---

## What Not to Say Yet

Avoid:

- "We have a guaranteed profitable wedding venue."
- "Parking is definitely sufficient."
- "We need millions now."
- "The design is already fixed."
- "This will open by a specific date."

Say instead:

- "The concept is promising."
- "The next step is professional validation."
- "The funding ask is for development and feasibility."
- "Final scale depends on survey, consent, and consultation."
