# Section 9: Case Studies & Lessons for Fernhill

**Status:** Complete (v3.0) | **Date:** May 2026 | **Evidence:** `research-output/case-studies/case-studies.json`

---

## 9.1 Purpose

Case studies demonstrate that Fernhill’s model — **charitable governance + heritage fabric + earned income** — is proven in Belfast and the UK. They also highlight mistakes to avoid (commercial-only models locking out future grants).

---

## 9.2 Belfast comparators

### Belfast Castle

- **Role:** Citywide heritage wedding benchmark; north Belfast location  
- **Package:** £1,110 (2026–27); in-house catering [S02]  
- **Lesson:** Proves citywide demand for heritage weddings  
- **Fernhill implication:** Replicate revenue logic with **independent governance** and **west Belfast** parkland location  

### Malone House

- **Role:** Format and pricing benchmark (Georgian BCC house)  
- **Package:** £1,044–£1,075; in-house catering [S03]  
- **Lesson:** Published BCC rates are the clearest public-sector fee benchmark  
- **Fernhill implication:** **Not** the same geography as Glencairn — use for pricing, not catchment equivalence

## 9.3 NI governance & funding models

### Clifton House Trust

- Grade A poorhouse; **CCNI charitable company**  
- Funders: NLHF, AHF, HED  
- **Lesson:** Direct governance template for Fernhill  

### The Duncairn Centre

- NLHF + **PEACE IV** + BCC + Arts Council  
- **Lesson:** Cross-community narrative unlocks SEUPB/PEACEPLUS; café anchor creates daily footfall  

### Crumlin Road Gaol

- 150,000+ visitors; NLHF 2011 award  
- **Lesson:** Commercial operator maximises freedom but **cannot** access future heritage capital — contrast supports charitable model  

---

## 9.4 UK precedent

### Wilton's Music Hall, London

- £4.5m phased restoration; charitable trust  
- Opened events **before** full restoration  
- **Lesson:** Authentic fabric is a marketing asset; early opening builds donor base  

---

## 9.5 Summary insights

1. Independent charitable trust is required for NLHF/AHF  
2. NLHF is anchor funder in every major case  
3. Weddings drive heritage house revenue in Belfast  
4. Multi-stream income (F&B, hire, events) builds resilience  
5. Cross-community framing is a NI funding multiplier  
6. Phased opening beats waiting for 100% completion
