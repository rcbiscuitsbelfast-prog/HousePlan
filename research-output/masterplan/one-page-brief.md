## The Opportunity

Fernhill House is a Grade B2 listed Victorian villa in Glencairn Park, west/northwest Belfast. The building has architectural and historic significance and is associated with Belfast merchant history, the former People's Museum, and the 1994 Loyalist ceasefire announcement. It is a rare heritage asset in a parkland setting with existing access, public transport, and potential to support community-led regeneration.

## The Proposed Vision

Restore Fernhill House as a **community-led heritage venue** combining:

- Heritage interpretation and learning.
- Tea room and daily public access.
- Community room hire.
- Sensitive wake / funeral tea provision.
- Small-to-medium weddings and private events.
- Skills, volunteering, and local supplier opportunities.

The aim is not to privatise parkland or create a high-volume commercial venue. The model is a heritage regeneration project with earned income to support long-term maintenance.

## Why It Matters

- Saves and activates a listed building.
- Supports Glencairn and west/northwest Belfast place-making.
- Provides public access and learning.
- Creates local jobs, volunteering, and supplier spend.
- Builds a sustainable income model rather than relying permanently on subsidy.
- Aligns with active travel, green-space, inclusion, and heritage regeneration objectives.

## Recommended Governance

The preferred structure is a **Charitable Company Limited by Guarantee** with a wholly-owned trading subsidiary for commercial operations. This protects community benefit, improves grant eligibility, and separates charitable purpose from trading risk.
