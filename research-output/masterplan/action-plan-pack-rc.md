# Action Plan Pack — Project Lead (R.C.)

**Purpose:** Personal sequencing guide for engagement, documents, and dependencies.  
**Companion document:** `fernhill-feasibility-study.md`  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Note:** Names of elected members are examples commonly referenced for **Castle DEA / west Belfast** context — **confirm current ward boundaries and representatives** before addressing correspondence.

---

## How to use this pack

1. Work **top to bottom** in Phase order unless a dependency blocks you.  
2. For each meeting, bring **one page** (project summary + three asks).  
3. Log outcomes in a simple **engagement register** (date, attendee, commitments, next step).

---

## Phase A — Positioning (Weeks 1–4)

### A1: Internal clarity (you + steering nucleus)

**Deliverables**

- One-page project summary (vision, site, charitable intent, “ask” is surveys/stage-1 funding only).  
- Roles matrix (who chairs; who handles finance; who handles council liaison).

**Dependencies**

- Before council politics: agree **steering group** mandate (advisory vs interim trustee path).

**Risks**

- Premature public promises on opening dates.

---

### A2: Belfast City Council — Members

**Who (examples — verify current office-holders)**

- **Frank McCoubrey** and other councillors covering **Castle Electoral Area** (and adjacent where Glencairn interests overlap).

**When**

- After one-page brief exists; ideally **before** first officer meeting so members can sponsor introductions.

**Documents**

- One-pager; map screenshot; link to feasibility study; listing citation [S01].

**What to say**

- Fernhill is **listed**, **in Glencairn Park**, proposed as **community-led** heritage venue (not residential conversion).  
- You seek **no assumptions** — you want **condition survey** and **planning pre-application pathway**.  
- Ask: **introduction** to Parks / Estates / Heritage / Regeneration leads.

**Dependencies**

- Councillors rarely commission surveys — they **unlock officer access**.

**Risks**

- Confusion with other capital schemes — keep Fernhill physically anchored (Glencairn Road / BT13).

---

### A3: Belfast City Council — Officers

**Who**

- **Parks** (Glencairn Park asset management).  
- **Estates / Property** (if applicable to municipal land interfaces).  
- **Planning** (pre-application — listed building + park context).  
- **Heritage / Conservation liaison** (often via planning or city heritage officer roles — titles vary).

**When**

- Week 2–6, after member introduction if possible.

**Documents**

- Feasibility study PDF extract (Sections 2–5).  
- Questions list (access, parking survey permission, operational constraints).

**What to ask**

- Who **owns** which interface around Fernhill (roads, paths, parking bays)?  
- What evidence do officers need for **event-day management** in parkland?  
- Process for **pre-application** with **HED** involvement.

**Dependencies**

- Officer clarity before major NLHF narrative claims about land/parking.

**Risks**

- Siloed departments — request **single coordinating officer** or joint meeting.

## Phase B — Statutory & funding architecture (Weeks 4–12)

### B1: Historic Environment Division (HED)

**Who**

- Department for Communities — listed building consent pathway.

**When**

- Before design freezes; ideally before significant survey expenditure.

**Documents**

- Heritage statement outline; photographs; listing record.

**What to ask**

- Scope of **heritage impact** documentation for proposed interventions.  
- Expectations for **recording** and **method statements**.

---

### B2: Architectural Heritage Fund (AHF) — NI programme

**Who**

- AHF NI programme manager / development grants contact (via ahfund.org.uk).

**When**

- When governance skeleton + survey specification exists.

**Documents**

- Development funding application (PDG-style): scope, budget, outcomes, match strategy outline.

**What to ask**

- Eligibility of **your legal vehicle** for stage-1 funding.  
- Acceptable **survey specification** and fee levels.

**Dependencies**

- Legal entity status often expected — confirm with AHF.

---

### B3: National Lottery Heritage Fund — NI team

**Who**

- NI engagement staff (Heritage Fund Belfast office — confirm current contacts via heritagefund.org.uk).

**When**

- Early relationship call; formal stage gates later.

**Documents**

- Project enquiry form / draft outcomes; engagement summary.

**What to ask**

- Programme fit (**Heritage Places** vs **Project Grants**) for your phased capital.  
- Match funding expectations and **evidence** standards.

---

### B4: NI Housing Executive — Place Shaping

**Who**

- Place shaping contacts (see NIHE “Place shaping contacts” page; general enquiries **03448 920 900** / info@nihe.gov.uk).

**When**

- When articulating **community benefit** and neighbourhood connectivity (Glencairn Place Shaping alignment).

**Documents**

- Short spatial narrative tying Fernhill to **housing/placemaking** priorities (transport section).

**What to ask**

- Who covers **Glencairn / west Belfast** partnership working; relevant **local partnership** forums.
