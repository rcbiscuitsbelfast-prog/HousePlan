# Fernhill House Two-Page Brief

**Audience:** Councillors, Belfast City Council officers, AHF/NLHF early engagement, potential trustees  
**Project lead:** R.C.  
**Document status:** Pre-application / feasibility development brief  

---

## 1. Project Summary

Fernhill House is a Grade B2 listed Victorian villa in Glencairn Park, Glencairn Road, Belfast BT13 3PT. It is a significant west/northwest Belfast heritage asset with architectural, social, and civic value. The proposed project is to restore and reactivate the building as a **community-led heritage venue** with a balanced mix of public access, learning, community use, remembrance, and earned income.

The project is currently at **early feasibility** stage. The correct next step is not a final capital campaign, but a structured development phase that can produce the technical evidence funders and council officers will require.

## 2. Proposed Uses

The proposed model includes:

- A daytime tea room and public access offer.
- Heritage interpretation and learning activity.
- Community room hire and small civic events.
- Wakes / funeral teas delivered with dignity and privacy.
- Small-to-medium weddings and private functions.
- Volunteer, skills, and local supplier opportunities.

The operating model must be designed to avoid conflict between daily visitors, park users, wedding guests, wake attendees, and suppliers. The design team should therefore test separated flows, event scheduling rules, delivery routes, and back-of-house requirements.

## 3. Strategic Fit

The project aligns with:

- Listed building protection and heritage-led regeneration.
- Belfast Local Development Plan objectives around sustainable place-making, design quality, and community infrastructure.
- Glencairn place-shaping priorities around connectivity, amenity, inclusion, and community services.
- Active travel and green infrastructure through Glencairn Park and Forth Meadow Community Greenway.
- Cross-community access and cultural interpretation, if embedded properly in governance and programming.

## 4. Access and Transport

The site benefits from existing access via Glencairn/Forthriver routes, an established park setting, nearby bus access including Metro 11C, and distributed parking potential. Initial observations suggest combined parking capacity of around 42-57 vehicles before optimisation. This must be verified by a measured parking/access survey and discussed with Belfast City Council Parks, Estates, Planning, and transport/access officers.

The preferred approach is **landscape-sensitive transport management** rather than large new hardstanding. Event-day demand should be managed through capacity limits, accessible priority spaces, shuttle/taxi coordination, car-share encouragement, and staggered arrivals/departures.

## 5. Governance and Delivery Model

The recommended structure is:

- **Fernhill Heritage Trust**: charitable company limited by guarantee.
- **Fernhill House Trading Ltd**: wholly-owned trading subsidiary for weddings, events, tea room, bar, and non-primary-purpose trading.

This model supports public benefit, protects the asset, improves eligibility for heritage grants, and allows commercial activity to be managed transparently.

## 6. Funding Route

The recommended funding pathway is:

1. Establish steering group and eligible development vehicle.
2. Seek AHF viability/development support.
3. Commission professional surveys and options appraisal.
4. Undertake BCC/HED pre-application engagement.
5. Prepare NLHF project enquiry / expression of interest.
6. Assemble match funding from HEF, trusts, philanthropy, council/community sources, and other public programmes where eligible.

Likely funders to test include AHF, NLHF, Historic Environment Fund NI, Community Foundation NI, philanthropic trusts, and relevant Belfast/community programmes.

## 7. Development Phase Outputs Needed

The development phase should produce:

- Building condition survey.
- Measured access and parking survey.
- Heritage Impact Assessment scope.
- Planning and listed building consent route.
- Options appraisal.
- QS cost plan.
- Business plan and financial model.
- Community engagement report.
- Governance documents.
- Funding strategy and match funding plan.

## 8. Immediate Ask

R.C. is seeking:

- Councillor support for officer introductions.
- Belfast City Council officer meeting across Parks, Estates, Planning, Heritage/Conservation, and Regeneration.
- Advice on council land/asset interfaces and event-management constraints.
- Initial AHF/NLHF guidance on development funding eligibility.
- Permission and support to scope surveys and pre-application work.

## 9. Key Risks to Manage

- Condition and capital cost uncertainty.
- Planning/listed building consent risk.
- Event parking and neighbour concerns.
- Over-optimistic revenue assumptions.
- Weak governance if steering group is not properly skilled.
- Community trust if consultation is late or tokenistic.

## 10. Recommended Decision

Support a **time-limited development phase** to test the proposal professionally. This phase should confirm whether Fernhill can move from concept to capital-ready project.
