# Heritage Venue Case Studies — Research Report
*case-study-agent v2.0.0 | 2026-05-08 | POPULATED with live research — DO NOT OVERWRITE*

---

## Summary Pattern

Six cases examined: four in Belfast/NI, two UK comparators. Consistent pattern:
- **Charitable trust governance** = dominant model (required for NLHF, AHF, HEF NI)
- **NLHF** = anchor funder in every case without exception
- **Multi-revenue streams** = essential for financial resilience
- **Cross-community narrative** = NI funding multiplier (SEUPB access)
- **Phased opening + early community engagement** = stronger outcomes than waiting for completion

---

## Case Study 1 — Belfast Castle
*Victorian baronial castle (1870), Grade B+, Cave Hill, Belfast | BCC managed*

- **10-hour wedding package:** £1,071 (April 2025–March 2026) [1]
- **Ceremony:** ~100 guests | **Reception:** ~200 guests
- **Revenue streams:** Weddings (primary), events, restaurant, heritage tours
- **Weekend occupancy:** High — consistently booked months ahead

**Lesson for Fernhill:** Proof of concept. BCC pricing is subsidised and capped. Independent Fernhill can price at 100–230% premium while still below commercial hotel rates. Heritage narrative generates self-referral bookings.

---

## Case Study 2 — Malone House
*Georgian country house (c.1820), Grade B1, Barnett Demesne, south Belfast | BCC managed*

- **10-hour wedding package:** £1,044 (2025–26); £1,075 (2026–27) [1][2]
- **Harberton Room:** £105/hr (before 6pm); £115/hr (after 6pm); daytime block £505 [2]
- **Ceremony:** ~80 | **Reception:** ~150 | **In-house catering available**
- **Weekend occupancy:** Routinely fully booked — booked months ahead

**Lesson for Fernhill:** Closest geographic comparator — same south Belfast catchment. Published pricing is the clearest market benchmark available. High occupancy confirms real, proven, unmet demand in Fernhill's target market.

## Case Study 3 — Clifton House, Belfast
*Grade A listed poorhouse (1774), North Queen Street | Clifton House Trust (CCNI charitable company)*

- **Funders:** NLHF, AHF, Historic Environment Division NI
- **Est. total investment:** £2m–£4m across multiple campaigns [3]
- **Governance:** CCNI-registered Charitable Company — **direct model for Fernhill**

**Lesson for Fernhill:** NLHF funds across multiple campaigns. Charitable trust governance essential. Community history narrative drives fundraising more than architectural merit alone.

---

## Case Study 4 — The Duncairn Centre, Belfast
*Grade B1 former Presbyterian church | Duncairn Community Partnership | Opened 2014, capacity 170*

- **Funders:** NLHF, SEUPB PEACE IV, BCC, Arts Council NI
- **Est. project cost:** £3m–£4m [4]
- **Revenue streams:** Arts events, rehearsal hire, community hire, café

**Lesson for Fernhill:** NLHF + SEUPB combination is proven in Belfast. Cross-community narrative is the key to unlocking SEUPB. Café as community anchor — essential even at low margins.

---

## Case Study 5 — Crumlin Road Gaol, Belfast
*Grade B+ Victorian gaol (1846, Sir Charles Lanyon) | Private commercial operator | 150,000+ visitors/year*

- **Funders:** NLHF (2011 award) [5][6]
- **Revenue streams:** Heritage tours (primary), weddings, events, Halloween events

**Lesson for Fernhill:** Heritage tourism viable in Belfast. Private commercial operation = locked out of future NLHF/AHF capital. Fernhill's charitable model preserves ongoing public investment access.

---

## Case Study 6 — Wilton's Music Hall, London
*Grade II* Victorian music hall (1858) | Wilton's Music Hall Trust | £4.5m phased restoration*

- **Funders:** NLHF, Arts Council England, Historic England, private donors
- **Key strategy:** Deliberate "beautiful ruin" aesthetic — exposed brick, peeling plaster became brand asset
- **Approach:** Phased opening — events hosted while restoration still in progress

**Lesson for Fernhill:** Open early, open authentically. Don't wait for full restoration. Fernhill's genuine Victorian fabric is a commercial asset. Phased opening builds income, community, and donor momentum before the capital campaign completes.
