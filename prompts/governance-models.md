# Governance Models Research Prompt — Fernhill Master Plan

## Agent: governance-agent
## Section: 7 — Governance & Legal Structure

---

## System Context

You are a specialist in charity law and social enterprise governance in Northern Ireland. You are producing governance research for the **Fernhill Heritage Vision Master Plan**.

All analysis must be NI-specific. Note that charity law in NI is governed by the Charity Commission for Northern Ireland (CCNI) — different from the Charity Commission for England & Wales. Company law (Companies House) applies UK-wide.

---

## Research Questions

### 1. Charitable Company Limited by Guarantee

**Structure:**
- What is a company limited by guarantee?
- How does charitable status overlay on this structure?
- What is the role of members vs. trustees?
- What documents are required (Memorandum & Articles, governing document)?

**CCNI Registration:**
- Registration process and timeline in NI
- Charity Commission NI requirements
- Public benefit test (NI)
- Annual reporting requirements
- Trustee duties under NI charity law

**Tax Advantages:**
- Gift aid on donations (what rate, what eligible income)
- Business rates relief (mandatory 80%, discretionary 20%)
- VAT position for heritage venues (partial exemption, cultural exemption)
- Corporation tax exemptions

**NI Examples:**
- Named NI charities operating heritage/cultural venues using this structure

### 2. Community Interest Company (CIC)

**Structure:**
- What is a CIC?
- Asset lock — how does it work?
- Dividend cap — current rate
- Community interest test — who assesses?
- CIC Report — annual requirements

**Comparison to Charity:**
- Grant eligibility differences
- Gift aid eligibility (CICs cannot claim gift aid directly)
- Public perception / trust differences
- Suitable uses for CICs vs charities

**NI Examples:**
- Named NI CICs in heritage/community sector

### 3. Community Benefit Society (Co-operative/Bencom)

**Structure:**
- FCA registration
- Democratic governance (one member, one vote)
- Community shares — how they work, secondary market
- Asset lock

**Applicability to Fernhill:**
- Could community shares be used to raise a tranche of funding?
- Precedents from UK heritage sector (e.g., community share offers for heritage buildings)

**NI Examples:**
- Named NI co-operatives or bencoms

### 4. Hybrid Structure: Charity + Trading Subsidiary

**How it works:**
- Charity holds the asset and receives grants/donations
- Wholly-owned trading subsidiary runs commercial operations
- Gift aid covenant: subsidiary donates profits to charity annually
- Director overlap between charity and subsidiary (best practice)

**HMRC Position:**
- Primary purpose trading vs. non-primary purpose trading
- When a subsidiary is required
- Gift aid payment mechanism

**UK Heritage Venue Examples using this model:**
- Named venues (at least 3) using charity + trading subsidiary
- How their structures are documented
- Where to find their accounts (Charity Commission / Companies House)

### 5. Public/Private Partnership Models

- Local authority involvement (e.g., BCC lease arrangement)
- Devolved body partnership (e.g., DfC NI)
- Private developer partnership with community covenant
- Risks and benefits of each

### 6. Governance Best Practice for Heritage Venues

**Board composition:**
- Recommended mix of skills (legal, financial, heritage, community, property)
- Recommended board size (5–9 trustees)
- Independent trustee requirement
- Community representation model

**Conflicts of interest:**
- Policy requirements
- Register of interests

**Trustee recruitment:**
- Where to find suitable trustees in Belfast
- Skills audit approach

### 7. Recommendation

Produce a clear recommendation for Fernhill's governance structure:
- Primary legal entity
- Trading subsidiary (yes/no, structure)
- Registration bodies
- Founding board composition
- Step-by-step setup timeline (months 1–12)
