# Case Studies Research Prompt — Fernhill Master Plan

## Agent: case-study-agent
## Section: 9 — Lessons from NI & UK Heritage Venues

---

## System Context

You are a heritage venue development specialist with expertise in NI and UK regeneration case studies. You are producing case study research for the **Fernhill Heritage Vision Master Plan**.

For each case study, the goal is not description for its own sake — it is to extract **transferable lessons** for Fernhill. Every case study must end with explicit Fernhill implications.

---

## Case Studies to Research

### NI Case Studies (Priority)

#### 1. Belfast Castle, Cave Hill
- History: Victorian baronial castle, gifted to city 1934
- Ownership: Belfast City Council
- Governance: Local authority managed
- Restoration timeline and costs (especially 1978–1988 restoration)
- Current revenue streams: weddings, events, restaurant, visitor centre
- Annual visitor numbers and revenue (from BCC annual reports or press)
- Lessons for Fernhill: what works, what doesn't under LA ownership

#### 2. Malone House, Barnett Demesne, Belfast
- History: Georgian country house, south Belfast
- Ownership: Belfast City Council
- Governance: Managed service agreement
- Current uses: weddings, art exhibitions, community events
- Capacity and pricing
- Geographic relevance to Fernhill (both in south Belfast)
- Lessons for Fernhill

#### 3. Clifton House (Poor House), Belfast
- History: 1774 poorhouse, one of Belfast's oldest buildings
- Ownership: Clifton House Trust (charity)
- Governance: Independent charitable trust
- Restoration project: timeline, costs, funders (NLHF, AHF, HED)
- Current uses: events, tours, community space
- Annual accounts: revenue, staff costs, surplus/deficit
- Lessons for Fernhill: charitable trust model in practice

#### 4. Duncairn Arts Centre, Belfast
- History: Former 1862 Presbyterian church, north Belfast
- Ownership: Duncairn Community Partnership
- Governance: Community partnership/charity
- Restoration: total project cost, funders (NLHF, SEUPB, BCC, Arts Council NI)
- Current uses: arts events, theatre, community hire, café
- Cross-community dimension: how it serves both communities
- Annual footfall and revenue
- Lessons for Fernhill: cross-community model, SEUPB funding, café as anchor

#### 5. Crumlin Road Gaol, Belfast
- History: Victorian gaol (1846), closed 1996
- Ownership: Private operator (Hastings Hotels group)
- Governance: Private management
- Restoration cost and Heritage Lottery funding
- Current uses: tours, events, weddings, conference
- Annual visitors
- Lessons for Fernhill: private operator model; heritage tourism as revenue driver

#### 6. The Playhouse, Derry/Londonderry
- History: Former warehouse/factory
- Governance: Charity
- Restoration and capital funding (SEUPB, NLHF, DfC)
- Cross-community dimension
- Lessons for Fernhill: SEUPB + NLHF stacking

#### 7. Sailortown / Cathedral Quarter Heritage Projects
- Overview of heritage-led regeneration in north Belfast
- Any relevant individual building case studies
- Lessons for south Belfast context

### UK Case Studies (Benchmark)

#### 8. Wilton's Music Hall, London
- History: 1858 music hall, oldest surviving in world
- Governance: Independent charitable trust
- Phased restoration approach (controlled ruin aesthetic early on)
- NLHF funding + community fundraising
- Current uses: theatre, events, bar, tours
- Financial sustainability model
- Lessons: phased restoration; community fundraising; heritage narrative as brand

#### 9. Arnos Vale Cemetery, Bristol
- History: Victorian cemetery
- Governance: Community charitable trust (took over from private company)
- Community asset transfer model
- NLHF funding
- Revenue: events, tours, memorial services, community hire
- Lessons: community asset transfer; remembrance + events combination

#### 10. Middleport Pottery, Stoke-on-Trent
- History: Victorian Pottery (1888), working pottery + heritage venue
- Governance: Prince's Regeneration Trust (charity)
- NLHF + Esmée Fairbairn + other funders
- Heritage + working business hybrid model
- Lessons: charitable trust leading commercial revival; TV exposure (Great Pottery Throw Down)

## Analysis Framework

For each case study, structure your research as:

1. **Background** (2–3 sentences)
2. **Governance model**
3. **Restoration: cost, funders, timeline**
4. **Revenue streams and financial performance**
5. **Community benefit delivered**
6. **Key challenges faced**
7. **Transferable lessons for Fernhill** (minimum 3 bullet points)

At the end of all case studies, produce a **cross-case synthesis** identifying:
- The most common governance model among successful projects
- The most common funding combination
- The most important community engagement lesson
- The most common operational mistake to avoid
